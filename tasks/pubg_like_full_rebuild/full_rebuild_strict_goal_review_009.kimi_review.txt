### Remaining Loopholes (10)

1. **Empty scaffold packets (12/12)** — All production packets are directory-only. A `.gitkeep` or tiny corrupt file would pass structural checks.
2. **`probe_only` boundary slip** — The TRELLIS+TextureAlchemy crate (5 maps, Blender evidence) is close to the production boundary and could be rhetorically upgraded without meeting LOD, collision proxy, or gameplay evidence requirements.
3. **Zero reference images** — 11 prompt markdown files exist but no actual images. W2 could be falsely claimed complete by pointing to prompts.
4. **Shape-only as "complete chain"** — `hunyuan_shape_demo_001` has Blender evidence but 0 texture maps. Could be claimed as "reaching Three.js."
5. **"Removed from slice" escape hatch** — Hard assets (hero rifle, characters) could be dropped from requirements without genuine production attempts.
6. **Empty runner outputs not caught** — `009.kimi_review.txt` and `010.kimi_worker.txt` are empty files. No gate verifies runner output is meaningful.
7. **Quality-blind texture counting** — Filename pattern matching (`basecolor.png`) doesn't verify resolution, format validity, or non-solid-color content.
8. **Blocked-route-as-tested** — A route can satisfy "tested" with a superficial note. No automated verification of failure evidence depth.
9. **Aggregate vs per-asset** — 8/12 PBR and 12/12 evidence are aggregate thresholds. A lopsided distribution (all clutter done, no hero rifle) could hit counts.
10. **No texture resolution gate** — A 64x64 and a 2048x2048 basecolor count equally.
